Passing, setting and spike

  • Pass: The initial contact made by a player to control the incoming serve or hit. This is typically executed using the forearms and is crucial for setting up the next play.
  • Set: The second touch of the ball, often performed with the fingertips to position it for a spike. A well-executed set creates scoring opportunities for the team.
  • Spike: The powerful downward hit delivered by an attacking player to send the ball forcefully into the opponent's court. Timing and technique are essential for a successful spike.
